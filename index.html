IPSEGO

  • Company: Funkadelichik
  • Date: 2015-2016
  • Job: Game Design

The idea & ambition behind that 6-month project was quite the challenge : design a game that reach the player about her-his principles/values in order to let her/him discover or confirm them.

After a few meetings, we decided to make a point'n'click adventure. Narrative Design was, at that moment, the quickest and most efficient way to play & experience those values.

Previous Next

We quickly decided on the core concept: the player is sent to explore an extra dimensional world where the inhabitants are living representations of groups of principles based on the work of Shalom H. Swchartz. By interacting with them, making choices, s-he will discover her/his own values.

Orleanoide

  • Company: Funkadelichik
  • Date: 2014
  • Job: Game Design & Programmer

One month length project for the science festival of Orleans. The constraints were to realize a game that uses the festival's mascot - the orleanoid -, a popular core gameplay and introduce a serious aspect in it.

Previous Next

Therefore we proposed an dual stick shooter with a little narrative twist. The player shoots some unidentified enemies in order to save little friendly orleanoids. If s-he messed up or saved enough of them, the great One asks him/her a question about the ongoing festival.


Tower Defense

  • Company: Funkadelichik
  • Date: 2014
  • Job: Game Design & Programmer

This game was born from an intern challenge. 3 weeks to design 3 projects. That was one of them. It was designed & built around one of our client's important theme: Pollution. More precisely: Waste Sorting.

Waste sorting is all about different ways to collect & treat trash. I had to imagine which existing core gameplay would be capable to represent the abstract system of waste sorting.

Previous Next

Tower Defense was the answer. That's how you obtain a game where you have to install lovely little waste treatment plants that try to stop waves of different types of trashes to end in the open dump. Save your town !

WATER TREATMENT

  • Client: Funkadelichik
  • Date: 2014
  • Job: Game Design

This game was made for the French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation or INRIA (in french). They asked us to conceive a game based on their research on water treatment for college students.

Previous Next

The player have to test different settings and find the best one to treat as fast as possible the water of multiple lakes. To make it more real, the game was based upon the simulation run by INRIA for the lakes. Those simulation takes the form of video showing off the exact behavior of the water under various settings.
